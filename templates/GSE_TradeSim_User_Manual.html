1. Introduction

GSE TradeSim, developed for the Young Investors Network (YIN), is a web-based platform designed to enhance financial literacy by allowing users to practice trading stocks listed on the Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE) in a risk-free environment.

Purpose

This manual provides comprehensive guidance for users, including students, educators, and aspiring investors, to navigate and utilize the platform effectively.

System Requirements

  • Web Browser: Google Chrome, Firefox, or Safari (latest versions recommended).
  • Internet Connection: Required for hosted versions.
  • Access URL: http://localhost:5001 for local deployment or the URL provided by your administrator.
  • Flask Server: Ensure the server is running with flask run --port=5001 for local access.

2. Getting Started

Accessing the Platform

  1. Open a web browser and navigate to http://localhost:5001 or the provided URL.
  2. Ensure the Flask server is running in your terminal with flask run --port=5001.
  3. The Login page will appear if you are not signed in.

Creating an Account

  1. Click the Sign Up link on the login page.
  2. Enter a unique Username, Password, and Confirm Password.
  3. Click Sign Up. If successful, you will access the Market Dashboard. If an error occurs (e.g., "Username already exists"), choose a different username.

Screenshot: Sign-up page with fields for username, password, and confirm password, featuring YIN logo at the top.

Logging In

  1. Enter your Username and Password on the login page.
  2. Click Login to access the dashboard.
  3. If an error occurs (e.g., "Invalid username or password"), verify your credentials or sign up.

3. Navigating the Market Dashboard

Header

  • Logos: Young Investors Network and Ghana Stock Exchange logos.
  • Title: "GSE TradeSim" displayed prominently.
  • Market Time: Real-time clock updating every second.
  • User Info: Displays your username and a Logout button.
  • Theme Toggle: Moon/sun icon to switch between light and dark themes.

Screenshot: Header with YIN and GSE logos, market time, username, and theme toggle icon.

Dashboard Statistics

  • Cash Balance: Virtual funds available for trading (initially 100,000 GHS).
  • Portfolio Value: Total value of cash and stock holdings.
  • Today’s Gain/Loss: Daily portfolio performance as a percentage.
  • Top Performer: Stock with the highest percentage change (e.g., "BOPP (+2.34%)").

4. Searching and Filtering Stocks

Search Input

  1. Enter a stock symbol (e.g., "BOPP") or name (e.g., "Benso Palm Plantation PLC") in the search bar.
  2. The Available Stocks table updates dynamically with matching results.

Sector Filter

  1. Select a sector (e.g., "Financial Services", "Telecom") from the dropdown.
  2. Choose "All Sectors" to view all stocks.

Sorting Stocks

  1. In the Available Stocks section, use the Sort by dropdown to order stocks by:
    • Name: Alphabetical order.
    • Price: Highest to lowest.
    • Change: Highest to lowest percentage change.

Screenshot: Available Stocks table with search bar, sector filter dropdown, and sort options.

5. Analyzing Stocks

  1. Select a stock from the Select a stock dropdown (e.g., "MTNGH - Scancom PLC").
  2. Choose a timeframe (1H, 1D, 1W) to view price history for the last hour, day, or week.
  3. The chart displays price movements in GHS, with green lines for gains and red for losses.
  4. Hover over the chart for detailed price data at specific points.

Screenshot: Stock Analysis section with stock selector, timeframe buttons (1H, 1D, 1W), and line chart.

6. Trading Stocks

Initiating a Trade

  1. Click the Trade button next to a stock in the Available Stocks or Portfolio table.
  2. A modal displays the stock’s symbol, name, price, and percentage change.

Configuring a Trade

  1. Select Buy or Sell from the Action dropdown. (Selling is only available for owned stocks.)
  2. Enter the number of Shares to trade.
  3. The Estimated Cost updates based on the current price and shares.

Confirming a Trade

  1. Click Confirm Trade.
  2. A success message (e.g., "Bought 50 shares of BOPP successfully!") appears, and the modal closes after 2 seconds.
  3. If an error occurs (e.g., "Insufficient funds"), adjust the shares and retry.

Screenshot: Trade modal showing stock details, action dropdown, shares input, and confirm button.

7. Managing Your Portfolio

Viewing Holdings

  • Symbol: Stock identifier (e.g., "BOPP").
  • Shares: Number of shares owned.
  • Avg. Cost: Average purchase price per share.
  • Current Value: Market value of holdings.
  • Gain/Loss: Profit or loss in GHS and percentage.

Selling Stocks

  1. Click Trade next to a stock in your portfolio.
  2. Select Sell, enter the number of shares, and confirm.

Resetting Your Portfolio

  1. Click the Reset button in the portfolio section.
  2. Confirm to reset your cash to 100,000 GHS and clear all holdings.

9. Understanding Stock Price Changes

In GSE TradeSim, stock prices change to simulate the dynamics of the real Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE), helping you learn how markets work in a risk-free environment. While the platform uses simulated data, the price movements are designed to mimic real-world factors. Here are the key factors that drive stock price changes:

Supply and Demand

  • How It Works: Prices rise when more users buy a stock (high demand) and fall when more sell (high supply).
  • In GSE TradeSim: The platform simulates this by adjusting prices based on virtual trading activity or algorithms, affecting stocks like MTN Ghana (MTNGH) or Benso Oil Palm Plantation (BOPP).

Company Performance

  • How It Works: In real markets, a company’s financial health (e.g., profits, revenue) impacts prices. Strong earnings increase prices, while losses decrease them.
  • In GSE TradeSim: Prices may reflect hypothetical events (e.g., a virtual “earnings report” for Ecobank Ghana). Check the Stock Analysis chart for trends.

Market Sentiment

  • How It Works: Investor confidence or news affects prices. Positive news boosts prices, while negative news lowers them.
  • In GSE TradeSim: Simulated news events (e.g., a contract for GOIL) influence prices. Monitor the Top Performer card.

Economic Factors

  • How It Works: Economic conditions like interest rates or inflation impact prices.
  • In GSE TradeSim: Simplified economic scenarios (e.g., a virtual “downturn”) affect prices, especially in sectors like Financial Services.

Sector and Industry Trends

  • How It Works: Industry-specific events (e.g., new telecom technology) affect related stocks.
  • In GSE TradeSim: Use the Sector Filter to see how stocks in Telecom or Agriculture move together.

Random Market Volatility

  • How It Works: Prices can fluctuate unpredictably due to market noise.
  • In GSE TradeSim: Controlled randomness simulates volatility. Track daily changes in the Today’s Gain/Loss card.

Using This in GSE TradeSim

  • Analyze Trends: Use the Stock Analysis chart (1H, 1D, 1W) to spot patterns.
  • Make Informed Trades: Check price history and sector performance before trading.
  • Learn and Experiment: Use the risk-free environment to test strategies. Reset your portfolio to try again.

Key Tip: Prices in GSE TradeSim are simulated to teach market dynamics. Practice here to build skills for real-world investing. Visit younginvestorcalculator.com to explore long-term investment impacts.

10. Logging Out

  1. Click the Logout button in the header.
  2. You will be redirected to the login page, with your portfolio saved for future sessions.

11. Troubleshooting

  • Cannot Log In: Verify credentials or sign up for a new account.
  • Insufficient Funds: Check your cash balance and reduce shares.
  • Chart Not Loading: Ensure a stock and timeframe are selected.
  • Server Errors: Confirm the Flask server is running (flask run --port=5001).
  • Contact Support: Visit yinvestors.org for assistance.
