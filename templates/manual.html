Introduction

GSE TradeSim, developed for the Young Investors Network (YIN), is a web-based platform that allows users to practice trading stocks listed on the Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE) in a completely risk-free environment using virtual money.

💰
GHS 1,000,000 Virtual Capital
Every trader starts with GHS 1,000,000 in virtual funds — zero real money at risk.
📊
Live GSE Stock Data
Real prices from the Ghana Stock Exchange, updated every 30 seconds when market is open.
🏆
Competition Mode
Compete with classmates on the leaderboard — grow your portfolio the most to win.
🔒
Risk Management Tools
Stop-loss orders and price targets teach real-world portfolio protection strategies.

05

Trading Stocks

Opening a Trade

  1. Find a stock in the Market view. Click the green Buy or red Sell button in the Action column.
  2. A trade modal appears showing the stock name, symbol, and current price.
  3. Use the Buy / Sell tabs at the top of the modal to switch between buying and selling.

Placing a Market Order (Standard)

1
Select Order Type: Market
This executes immediately at the current market price. This is the default option.
2
Enter Number of Shares
Type how many shares you want to buy or sell. The Order Summary updates automatically showing total cost.
3
Click "Execute Order"
The trade is confirmed instantly. A notification appears confirming the transaction and its value.
🚫
Market ClosedYou cannot trade when the market is closed. The admin must open the market first. The Buy/Sell buttons will show an error if you attempt to trade when closed.
⚠
Insufficient FundsIf your order value exceeds your available cash, the trade will fail. Reduce your share count or choose a cheaper stock.
06

Advanced Orders New

The platform supports three powerful order management tools to help you trade strategically, just like real professional investors do.

Limit Orders

A limit order lets you set a maximum price you're willing to pay when buying. The order only executes if the stock's current price is at or below your limit.

  1. In the trade modal, select Limit Order from the Order Type dropdown.
  2. Enter your Limit Price — the maximum price per share you'll accept.
  3. If the current price is above your limit, the order will be rejected with a message showing both prices.
💡
Example: MTN Ghana (MTNGH) is trading at GHS 3.20. You set a limit of GHS 3.00. The order is rejected because the current price exceeds your limit. When the price drops to GHS 3.00 or below, your order would be accepted.

Stop-Loss Orders

A stop-loss automatically sells your shares if the stock price falls to or below a price you set. This limits how much money you can lose on a position.

  1. When placing a buy order, enter a value in the Stop-Loss Price field.
  2. If the stock's price falls to that level, the system automatically sells all your shares.
  3. The sale is recorded as a stop_loss_sell transaction in your history.
  4. You will see a notification in the Alerts section.
✅
Best Practice: Set your stop-loss 5–10% below your purchase price to protect against large drops while allowing normal market fluctuations. This is a core risk management strategy used by real investors.

Price Targets

A price target alerts you when a stock reaches a price you're aiming for. Unlike stop-loss, it does NOT automatically sell — it notifies you so you can decide what to do.

  1. When placing a buy order, enter a value in the Price Target field.
  2. When the stock's price rises to or above your target, an alert is triggered.
  3. Check the Alerts section (🔔) to see all triggered price targets.
  4. You then decide whether to sell and take profit, or hold for higher gains.

Updating Existing Orders

You can update stop-loss and price target values for stocks you already own from the Portfolio view. The current settings are shown in the holdings table.

Managing Your Portfolio

Reading Your Holdings Table

Selling from Portfolio

  1. In the Portfolio view, click the red Sell button next to any holding.
  2. Enter the number of shares to sell and confirm.
  3. You cannot sell more shares than you own.

Resetting Your Portfolio

  1. Click the Reset Portfolio button in the Portfolio view (top right of holdings panel).
  2. Confirm the action in the popup. This cannot be undone.
  3. Your cash resets to GHS 1,000,000 and all holdings are cleared.
⚠
Think Before ResettingResetting clears your entire trade history and all holdings permanently. Only do this if you want to start fresh. In a competition, resetting may affect your leaderboard ranking.
08

Alerts System New

The Alerts panel (🔔 in the sidebar) tracks two types of automatic notifications:

Stop-Loss Alerts

Whenever the system automatically sells one of your positions due to a stop-loss trigger, an alert appears here. It shows: the stock symbol, number of shares sold, the trigger price, and the total sale value received.

Price Target Alerts

When a stock you're holding reaches your set price target, an alert appears here. The alert shows the stock symbol, your target price, and the current price. Note: you must manually decide to sell — price targets are notifications, not automatic sells.

💡
Alert BadgeA red number badge on the 🔔 icon in the sidebar and top bar shows how many unread alerts you have. Check it regularly during active trading.
09

Understanding Stock Price Changes

In GSE TradeSim, prices change to simulate the dynamics of the real Ghana Stock Exchange. Understanding why prices move is a core part of learning to invest.

How Prices Update

The platform operates in two modes:

Mode How It Works Frequency
Real Data Mode Fetches actual GSE prices from afx.kwayisi.org Every 30 seconds
Simulation Mode Applies ±2% random changes if real data unavailable Every 10 seconds

Supply and Demand

Prices rise when more investors want to buy a stock (high demand) and fall when more want to sell (high supply). On the real GSE, a stock like MTN Ghana (MTNGH) with millions of shares traded daily is heavily influenced by this.

Company Performance

Strong earnings reports, new contracts, or positive news push prices up. Losses, scandals, or poor results push them down. In TradeSim, use the price history chart to spot trends that may reflect simulated performance events.

Market Sentiment

Even without news, investor confidence affects prices. Fear leads to selling (prices drop); optimism leads to buying (prices rise). The GSE Composite Index (GSE-CI) tracks this overall market mood.

Sector Trends

Stocks in the same sector often move together. Use the Sector Filter in the Market view to observe how Financial Services or Energy stocks trend as a group.

Controlled Volatility

The simulation applies a controlled random factor (up to ±2% per cycle) to model real-world price noise. This teaches you that not every price movement has a fundamental reason — managing this uncertainty is part of investing.

💡
Key Investment InsightRather than reacting to every price tick, focus on the overall trend over time. Investors who trade based on fundamentals and long-term analysis tend to outperform those who react to short-term noise. Use the P&L column in your portfolio to track your actual performance.
10

Admin Guide Updated

The Admin Panel is for competition administrators (YIN instructors/facilitators) only. Log in with the admin username and password to access it.

Admin Dashboard Sections

Section Purpose
Overview Key stats: total users, active traders, total trades, average portfolio value
Leaderboard Full competition rankings sorted by portfolio value, with growth % and trade count
Users List of all registered participants with their portfolio performance
Market Data Live view of all 22 GSE stocks with price sparklines
Competition Control Open/close the market, reset the entire competition

Opening and Closing the Market

  1. Go to Competition Control in the Admin sidebar.
  2. Click Open Market to allow trading. Click Close Market to pause trading.
  3. The market status is visible to all users in real time via the sidebar status pill.
💡
Competition Setup Flow① Close market → ② Wait for all participants to register → ③ Open market at competition start time → ④ Close market at end time → ⑤ Review leaderboard for final rankings.

Resetting the Competition

  1. Go to Competition Control.
  2. Click Reset All Users & Portfolios.
  3. Confirm twice in the popup dialogs.
  4. All user accounts and portfolios are permanently deleted. Participants will need to re-register.
🚨
This action is irreversible. All user data, portfolios, and transaction history are permanently deleted. Only use this to start a new competition round.

Monitoring the Leaderboard

The leaderboard auto-refreshes every 30 seconds. It shows each participant's rank (🥇🥈🥉 medals for top 3), portfolio value, growth percentage vs. the GHS 1,000,000 starting capital, and a visual performance bar.

Troubleshooting

Problem Solution
Cannot log in Verify your username and password. Use the Sign Up page to create a new account if needed.
"Insufficient funds" Reduce the number of shares or choose a less expensive stock. Check your cash balance in the sidebar.
"Market is closed" Wait for the administrator to open the market. You can browse stocks but not trade.
Limit order rejected The current price is above your limit. Either raise your limit price or wait for the price to drop.
Prices not updating Refresh the page. If still not updating, the real GSE data source may be temporarily unavailable — simulation mode will be active.
Stop-loss not triggering Stop-losses only check every 10 seconds. Ensure the price has actually reached your stop-loss level.
Page shows an error Refresh the browser. If the error persists, contact your administrator.
💡
Need More Help?Contact YIN support at yinvestors.org or reach your competition administrator.
